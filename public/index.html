The Road to School

“There are people using books and notebooks for cooking, since fuel is so hard to find.
The sight breaks your heart.”
– Woman in Aleppo, Syria.

Syria has witnessed the largest wave of displacement in modern times.
Nearly 5 million refugees have fled the country.
Roughly 7 million people have been internally displaced.

Education has come under repeated fire throughout the conflict.

For the children living in Syria and particularly the Aleppo area, going to school is a coin toss with death.

Teachers hold classes in underground caves to shelter students from bombings.

The children who live in host countries as refugees are often not allowed to enroll in local schools.

or they have to work in order to support their families.
If they are in their early teens, chances are that they won’t go back to school again.

Schools which accept refugees can be too far from the refugee camps.

or the teaching materials are in a different language.

An estimated one million of school aged refugees are not attending school.

That number rises to 2.1 million children inside Syria.

As you were reading these lines, one school-aged Syrian child had to give up on school.

Would you be looking at this screen if you had left school at a similar age?

“I’ve been back at school for two months. On the first day back I felt that my life had become beautiful. I was so happy that I would learn and study and fulfil my dream. I want to become a children’s doctor.”
– Zahra, 9 years old

“I felt so happy when I heard I was going back to school. I finally had a hope that I could succeed through studying. I want to be an engineer when I grow up so I can help to rebuild Syria.”
– Ahmad, 13 years old
